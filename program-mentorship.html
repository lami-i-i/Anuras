Mentorship Program

One-on-one guidance from women thriving in STEAM fields

Learn From Those Ahead of You

Sometimes what you need most isn't a classroom—it's someone who's walked the path you're considering telling you what it's really like. That's what our Mentorship Program is about: connecting girls with women who are working in STEAM fields and can offer real guidance, real advice, and real encouragement.

Our mentors include engineers, designers, researchers, educators, and innovators from across Africa who believe in your potential and want to help you thrive.

What Mentorship Looks Like

📞 For Mentees (Girls Like You)

You'll be paired with an experienced mentor in a field that interests you. Typically, this involves:

  • Monthly check-ins (virtual or in-person)
  • Real-world advice on career paths
  • Help navigating challenges in school or projects
  • Introduction to opportunities and networks

For Mentors

Our mentors are passionate about giving back. They share:

  • Their career journey and lessons learned
  • Practical advice on education & skills
  • Access to opportunities and connections
  • Encouragement when things get tough

Mentorship Focus Areas

  • Career Planning - Understanding different STEAM careers, what skills you need, and how to get there
  • Skill Development - Guidance on learning specific technical or creative skills relevant to your goals
  • Confidence Building - Working through self-doubt, impostor syndrome, and the challenges unique to being a girl in STEAM
  • Personal Growth - Developing resilience, communication skills, and leadership qualities
  • Opportunity Access - Help identifying and applying for scholarships, internships, and other opportunities

Mentorship Structure

Format: One-on-one meetings (usually monthly, sometimes more frequent)

Duration: Typically 6-12 months, but can be flexible based on goals

Medium: Video calls, phone calls, or in-person depending on location and preference

Your Goals: At the start, you and your mentor will discuss what you want to get from the relationship—whether it's guidance on a specific career, help with a project, or just someone to talk to about your STEAM journey

Who Our Mentors Are

Our mentors are women working across all STEAM fields: software engineers, product managers, researchers, designers, professors, entrepreneurs, and more. They're from Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, and across Africa. Most importantly, they remember what it was like to be a girl questioning whether STEAM was "for them"—and they want to help you see that it absolutely is.

How to Get Matched

Step 1: Tell us about yourself—what interests you in STEAM? What are your goals? What challenges are you facing?

Step 2: We match you with a mentor whose experience and personality align with your needs and interests.

Step 3: Meet with your mentor for a first conversation to build a relationship and set shared goals.

Step 4: Start your mentorship journey with regular check-ins, guidance, and support.

Interested?

Whether you're looking to be mentored or are a woman in STEAM interested in mentoring the next generation, we'd love to hear from you.
